ASSET: The Void Crawler (Mark VII Survey Skiff)
A six-limbed maintenance vehicle for The Gut's treacherous tunnels. Magnetic adhesion lets it crawl walls, ceilings, and stalactite formations over absolute void. Cramped cockpit, amber-tinted viewport, sealed against toxic atmosphere. Micro-hydro-capacitors. Remote sensor relay guidance.

LOGBOOK ENTRY: Year 487 Post-Launch
Logged by: Mira Solheim, Junior Maintenance Technician, Third Rotation
Location: Junction 7-Gamma, The Gut (Root Weave), Sector 12

I can hear my heartbeat.

Only that. My heartbeat. The hiss of recycled air through helmet seals. Somewhere far below, the groan of metal as Oris adjusts thermal regulators. I am wedged in a Crawler no bigger than a coffin, between massive coolant pipes that thrum with the ship's vital fluids.

The viewport shows only darkness. Not night—night has color, warmth from amber windows. This is nothing. Drop a stone here and never hear it land.

Headlights cut two thin beams into the black, revealing rust-stained pipes and the faint glow of bioluminescent fungi in the junction box ahead. They shouldn't be here. That's why I came.

My hands are steady on the controls. My shoulders ache from holding still. The Crawler is magnetized to the pipes, but that half-second of adhesion fluctuation haunts me—a millimeter shift in the dark.

Oris's voice crackles through my helmet: "Sample kit deployed. Awaiting your return, Mira."

I think about the Pearl, four kilometers above. Sunlight. My mother's garden. Real soil.

Here, I'm an intruder in the ship's intestines. I take what Oris permits. I leave no trace.

The fungi glow softly, patient, alien. I reach out with my sampler, coaxing a fragment into the vial.

This is my purpose. To notice what the machine misses. To be human in the machine's domain.

The vial seals. I guide the Crawler back toward light, toward voices.

The void remains below me. Silent. Waiting.
