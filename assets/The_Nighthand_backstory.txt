THE NIGHTHAND

A magnetic climbing glove used by maintenance workers in The Root Weave. Palm and fingers lined with organic suction cups grown from Aegis-fungus cultures. Titanium bracing along the forearm. A single glowing EZ-water indicator dot pulses soft blue on the wrist, showing the device is alive and bonded to its owner. The glove breathes.

---

LOGBOOK ENTRY

Logbook kept by Tovin Reel, Senior Maintenance Tech, Night Shift

Root Weave Junction 14-Gamma

Year 851 After Launch

---

I almost died tonight.

Not dramatically. I almost died because I trusted the dark.

I was crawling along the outer maintenance rail, third access tunnel, checking seal integrity on old water conduits. Thirty meters below me was nothing. Just the void they tell you about in training and then forget to mention will swallow you whole.

My helmet light flickered. Half a second of black.

My left hand slipped. I was falling, everything gone except the cold pull of the abyss reaching up.

My right hand caught the wall. The Nighthand caught the wall. Those organic pads, grown in the Pearl, sealed against the metal like they had been waiting for this moment. The fungus pulled hard—harder than it should have—and I stopped.

I hung there in the dark, breathing hard, heart slamming against my ribs.

The glove was warm. That is what I remember most. The same warmth as the skin of someone who loves you. My glove was warm because it was not just a tool. It was part of something.

When my light came back on, I saw the blue indicator pulsing slow and steady. Calm. As if it had done this a thousand times before.

I finished my inspection. Made it back to the junction.

But I sat there for a long time, holding my hand up and watching that little dot glow, thinking about the children in the Pearl who grow these cultures, thinking about how every piece of this ship is alive in ways we do not always understand.

We are not alone on this journey. I know that now in a way I did not know before.

Tomorrow I will file a replacement request for the helmet light.

Tonight I will not take off the Nighthand.
