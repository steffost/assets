ASSET: The Pulse Chalice

A ceremonial drinking vessel used by workers in the Agro-Aquatic Ring. Made from translucent EZ-gel composite, it glows faintly cyan when filled with structured water. Workers drink from it at shift changes to synchronize their body's hydration with the ship's fluid systems. The rim is warm to the touch, heated by the user's own bioelectric signature during the ritual of drinking.

---

Logbook Entry: Day 847

Logged by Kael, Aquaculture Technician, Third Shift

The blue tanks hum beside me as I unclip the Pulse Chalice from my belt. My fingers are pruned from the humidity. The water inside shimmers, alive with that faint violet glow that means the EZ-structures are properly formed. I brought it from the morning refill station, carried it through the mist corridors for six hours, and now at last I drink.

The water tastes like nothing. Like clean. Like nothing at all.

Around me, the protein vats burble their slow digestion. Somewhere above, the Pearl's artificial sun is setting, and I imagine I can feel the shift in my chest. The Chalice pulses once against my lips, recognizing me, measuring my hydration levels, feeding the data back to Oris.

My mother used to do this same motion. She told me it connects us to the ship. That we are not passengers but partners. I used to think that was poetry.

Now, after eight hundred shifts, I think it is simply true.

The water goes down. The glow brightens briefly, then fades. I clip the Chalice back to my belt and return to the tanks.

Tomorrow we harvest. Tonight, I am part of the machine.

I do not know if that makes me less human, or more.
