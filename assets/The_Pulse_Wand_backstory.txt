ASSET: The Pulse Wand
Classification: Monitoring Tool - Cultivator Grade

A slender crystalline rod, roughly sixty centimeters in length, used by cultivators in The Pearl to monitor bioluminescent organisms. The tip contains a Sub-Orb fragment that resonates with Vitalis Flux, glowing brighter when organisms thrive and dimming when they need attention. Each cultivator receives one upon certification.

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook of Mira Solstrand, Third Rotation Cultivator
The Pearl, Section 7 - Inverted Myrtle Groves
Year 2,847 After Launch

I pressed the wand's tip against the hanging myrtle bark. The crystal flared that emerald green I have come to love, and I released a breath I hadn't known I was holding. For three days, this grove had unstable readings.

In the inverted forest, trees hang downward above me, roots brushing against what we call sky but is really just the cloud layer catching mist. Water drips constantly from the canopy, a soft percussion against my hood. The air is thick with moisture and earthy sweetness. Somewhere distant, a bird calls out, its song echoing strangely in this upside-down world.

The Pulse Wand is how we speak to the plants, learn what they need. My grandmother said the old Earth cultivators simply looked at their crops, touched the soil with their hands. Here, everything is more subtle. We read the light, the frequencies, the pulse of life itself.

When the reading stabilized, I smiled. The grove would recover. The myrtles would glow their soft blue tonight, and night-shift workers would have their gentle light to work by.

This is why I do what I do. Not for glory. For this single moment of harmony between what I tend and what tends me.
