ASSET: The Mosswell Personal Cultivator

DESCRIPTION: A compact, self-contained hydroponic growing unit designed for personal use in residential quarters. The Mosswell uses bioluminescent mycelium networks to maintain optimal growing conditions for herbs, medicinal plants, and small vegetables. Its curved ceramic housing features a transparent observation panel where users can watch their plants grow under soft, pulsing cyan light. A small EZ-water reservoir feeds the root systems through micro-capillary action, requiring refilling only once per week.

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook of Mira Voss, Textile Artisan
Location: Residential Block 7-C, The City
Date: Cycle 2,847

The Mosswell hums quietly on my windowsill, its cyan glow mixing with the amber light from the corridor outside. I placed it there three cycles ago, and already I can see the first leaves of the basil pressing against the glass. Small things, but they matter.

There is something about watching roots grow that makes the journey feel shorter. Down in the Pearl, they have entire forests. Up here in the City, we grow in inches. But the mosswell does not judge. It simply feeds, waters, and glows in its slow rhythm.

My daughter asked me today why we bother growing food when the synthesizers can make anything we need. I told her it is not about the basil. It is about remembering that things need time to become. That patience is not waiting. That the ship moves because we move with it.

The basil will be ready by tomorrow evening. I plan to cut it with the ceramic blade my grandmother gave me, the one marked with her name in old script. I will put it on bread with salt from the Processing Ring.

Some rituals keep us human.

The mosswell pulses. The roots drink. We continue.
