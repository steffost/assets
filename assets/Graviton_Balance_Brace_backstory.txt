ASSET: Graviton Balance Brace (GB-7)

Forearm-worn stabilization device. Reduces effective mass by 40% via localized anti-grav field. Auto-deploys on balance loss. Haptic warnings when structural integrity drops. Standard issue for workers above the void.

LOGBOOK ENTRY - Third Shift, Cycle 4721

The brace hums as I edge past the junction node. Below me: nothing. Just black that swallows my headlamp three meters down.

I hate this section. Narrow path. Old grating that groans under weight. But I hate the corridors more. Too many people.

Here, only the distant thrum of The Spine. My brace pulses yellow. Warning. Stress fractures ahead. Protocol says report and wait for repair crew. Six hours. The section could fail by then.

So I move faster. Hand on rail. Eyes forward. Forty percent lighter. If the grating gives, I will not fall. I will float. I will grab the emergency line.

Mira wears her brace loose. Last month, she fell through Sector Nine. The brace deployed late. She broke her ankle. Not the fall killed her. Two hours alone in the dark before her distress beacon registered.

Now she wears it tight. Does not joke anymore.

The brace pulses green. Safe crossing ahead.

I step forward and do not look down.
