ASSET DESCRIPTION:

The Vita-Pin is a small bio-mechanical device worn over the heart. A hybrid of organic and technological elements, it contains a stabilized micro-culture of Cladosporium sphaerospermum suspended in EZ-water gel harvested from the ship's Aegis hull. The organism responds to the wearer's heartbeat and emotional state, causing it to pulse with soft bioluminescent light. When critical stress, radiation exposure, or biological crisis strikes, it releases measured doses of EZ-stabilized nutrients through the skin. The casing—grown from fermented mycelium composite, hand-shaped over years of use—bears the owner's unique cellular patterns. Deeply personal, never shared, it carries the weight of a portable life support system.

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Dr. Anya Kowalski, Maintenance Technician, Third Shift

Root Weave, Junction 17-Alpha

Year 847 After Launch

---

The light under my collar has been flickering all night. I can feel it against my sternum, that faint pulse trying to sync with my heartbeat. It is not syncing. Something is wrong.

I found the breach three hours ago. Junction 17-Alpha, lower elbow—the kind of damage that takes months off your life just standing near it. Radiation readings spiking through the floor grating. I had to go down and patch it manually while the Pin screamed silent warnings through my skin.

The Root Weave is never quiet. Tonight it sounds like breathing. Like something vast and living, dreaming just beyond the walls, and we are all just parasites swimming through its lungs. I try not to think about it. I focus on the weld.

When I came back up, my Pin was cold. Four minutes until it warmed against my chest again. Four minutes of my heart working alone, unassisted, fighting the cold that seeps in from the void below.

My shift ends in two hours. I will go back to my quarters and hold this Pin in my palm and watch it glow—green when healthy, amber when tired, red when dying. Tonight it glows the color of old moss. The color of something trying very hard to stay alive.

I think I understand how it feels.

Final readings logged.
