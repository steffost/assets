ASSET: The Vitae Locket
Classification: Personal Devotional Object / Heirloom

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook kept by Vera Okonkwo, Sanitation Supervisor, Second Shift
The Root Weave, Junction 23-Gamma
Year 412 After Launch

---

The air down here tastes like cold metal and recycled nothing. My boots echo on the gitter walkway between secondary coolant pipes. Below me, the void swallows every sound. Above, Sector 4's lights are just a rumor.

I stopped at the last checkpoint to check my locket. My mother pressed it into my hands the morning I descended, thirty-one years ago. The copper was warm then, almost hot against my young fingers.

"Because it remembers the Pearl," she said. "Even when you are in the dark, it remembers."

The spore pulses now, the same rhythm it has always held. Scientists would tell me it is just biology—fungus metabolizing trace radiation into light. They would miss the point.

The loneliness in The Root Weave never fully fades. The knowledge that if I fell, no one would hear me scream. The locket does not fix that. But it sits against my chest like a second heart, warm and patient. I am not just a sanitation supervisor crawling through the ship's intestines. I am a daughter of the Pearl.

I unclasp the locket and hold it in my palm. Golden light spills across my weathered fingers. For a moment, I am not in the cold and silence. I am in The Pearl, standing in morning mist while sunlight filters through the canopy. I am my mother's daughter. I am Gaia's seed.

I close the locket, tuck it back under my collar, and keep walking. The work is never done, but neither, somehow, am I.

Not while this warmth holds.
