ASSET: The Breathstone

A Breathstone is a palm-sized orb of living bioluminescent fungus suspended in stabilized EZ-water gel. Grown from the Aegis-Strain Cladosporium sphaerospermum, each stone is uniquely tuned to its owner's physiology over years of use. The organism responds to the wearer's heartbeat and stress hormones, glowing green when calm, amber when tense, and pulsing red in distress. During Vitae Cultus meditation, the stone turns translucent white as the body enters deep resonance with Gaias frequencies. Breathstones are worn as pendants, placed in meditation chambers, or kept in homes as living indicators of spiritual and physical wellbeing.

Logbook Entry - Third Shift, Day 289

The amber glow of my Breathstone caught my eye again as I wiped the contamination from the tank panel. Three hours into my shift and the specimen cultures are still unstable. The Pearl's artificial sun has shifted to evening amber, painting everything in that familiar honey-warm light that always makes me think of Earth photographs.

My stone pulses amber too. I can feel it against my chest, warm from my body heat.

Mira, you are a wreck tonight.

I held the stone to my ear like grandmother taught me. The soft bioluminescence hummed against my palm, that gentle vibration like a second heartbeat. Around me, the fungal shelves glowed their soft cyan-green, and the humid air carried the earthy smell of growing things. The sound of distant waterfalls through the root weave below filled the silence.

I closed my eyes. Breathe. Think of Gaias.

Slowly, the amber faded to green. The stone settled into its steady rhythm, and I felt the tension leave my shoulders.

This is why we do it. This is why we carry the stones. To remember that we are part of something larger. That even here, in the metal belly of a ship a thousand years from home, we are alive. We are Gaias spores, reaching for the stars.

My stone glowed steady green as I returned to my work.
