ASSET NAME: The Whisper Reed

DESCRIPTION: A bio-acoustic listening device cultivated by Root Weave maintenance workers over years of personal use. The main body is a single stalk of Cladosporium sphaerospermum, grown in a custom ceramic mold and shaped through careful pruning until it forms a natural whistle roughly 18 centimeters long. The interior channels are lined with nanoceramic membranes that convert vibrations into amplified sound. Over time, the fungal mycelium bonds with the user's nervous system through the palm, creating a sympathetic resonance that lets them feel the ship's sounds as much as hear them. No two reeds are alike.

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook kept by Kell Vasquez, Maintenance Technician, Third Shift

Root Weave, Junction 12-Gamma

Year 412 After Launch

---

The junction box is cold against my forehead. I press the reed to my lips and exhale slow.

Somewhere below me, the Spine is coughing. A thousand liters of greywater dropping through black pipes, the sound traveling up through the metal and into my skull like a second heartbeat. I have heard that heartbeat every night for eleven years. I know when it is happy. I know when it is afraid.

The reed vibrates against my palm. The mycelium threads beneath my skin hum in answer. My own pulse syncs with the ship's pulse. For a moment I cannot tell which is which.

The air down here tastes like copper and old rain. My helmet light catches the condensation on the nearest root formation. Everything is grey and black and the faint emergency red of the pathway markers. The void waits below, patient and absolute.

But up here, pressed against the box, listening, I am not alone. The ship talks to me in groans and sighs. A pipe here is carrying too much pressure. A seal there is starting to fail. The reed tells me everything.

Tomorrow the repair crews will come. They will fix what needs fixing. And no one will know that the ship whispered first to me.

That is between me and the roots.

End of entry.
