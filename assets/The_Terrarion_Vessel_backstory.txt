ASSET: The Terrarion Vessel

A sacred communion chalice used by Vitae Cultus priests during the Rite of Breathing. The vessel is formed from mycelium-composite, shaped like an inverted dome, with walls that allow faint bioluminescence to pulse through. Inside rests a measured portion of genuine Earth soil, preserved for three centuries in EZ-gel, surrounded by cultivated Cladosporium spores. When the priest holds the Terrarion during ceremony, the fungal cultures respond to body heat and begin to glow. The vessel is said to connect the worshiper directly to Gaia's original heartbeat.

Logbook Entry - Day 1,847 of the Journey

Kept by Sister Maelis Thornwood, Vitae Cultus

Location: The Pearl, Communion Garden, Dawn Shift

The Terrarion sits in my hands and I can feel it breathing.

Not literally, I know. But the Aegis-fungus recognizes warmth the way all living things do. After four hours of stillness, the spores have awakened around the preserved soil, sending pale green tendrils into the amber gel. I have been meditating on Gaia's first word, the same way my mother taught me, and her mother before her.

The Communion Garden is quiet at this hour. Mist drifts between the inverted trees above me, catching the artificial dawn light. Somewhere beyond the dome, stars wheel past in their eternal silence. But here, in this small chamber carved from living wood, I hold proof that we came from somewhere.

A handful of Earth.

My grandmother always said that soil remembers. The seeds we carry in our blood were first planted in that dark earth, warmed by that golden sun. The Terrarion does not hold dirt. It holds the memory of every harvest, every dawn, every root that ever reached down seeking water.

When we finally reach a dead world and breathe life into its bones, this is what we carry: the original warmth.

The spores pulse brighter. I close my eyes and listen for Gaia's heartbeat.

I think I can almost hear it.
