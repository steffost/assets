ASSET: The Mooder's Lace

A woven cord worn around the wrist, grown from Aegis-fungus mycelium and threaded with EZ-water micro-channels. The lace responds to the wearer's bioelectrical signature, shifting through colors to indicate stress, fatigue, and proximity to Vitalis energy fields. Maintenance workers in the Root Weave call it their "second skin" - a living warning system that speaks in hues.

---

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook kept by Pavel Merin, Pipe Technician, Third Shift
Junction 9, Root Weave
Year 853 After Launch

---

The lace around my wrist is turning grey. Not the good grey of morning fog over EZ Aqua, but the hungry kind - the shade of Entropy Ash before it hardens. My hands know this color before my eyes do.

I've been down in the Weave for six hours now. The tunnel walls sweat rust-colored moisture. Somewhere below me, the void waits with its patient nothing. Above, the city breathes its ten thousand lungs, but down here there is only the distant heartbeat of stabilizers and the drip of condensation keeping time.

The Mooder's Lace tells me what the sensors cannot - that I am standing in a place where the hull plating is thinner than it should be. The readings are acceptable. The lace disagrees. It knows about the micro-fracture three meters to my left, the one that Oris has not detected yet.

I mark the spot and move on, following the lifeline back through Junction 9. Behind me, the dark grows teeth. Ahead, a shaft of amber light marks the way to Sector 4 - to people and noise and the illusion of solid ground.

The lace pulses once, warm green now. We are past the danger.

We.

It is strange how quickly a person begins to speak for the lace. As if it were crew. As if it had a stake in whether I return.

I tighten the cord and keep walking.
