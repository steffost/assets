ASSET: Myco-Carafe (The Living Preserver)

A living vessel grown from genetically modified mycelium, cultivated in The Pearl's canopy laboratories. The carafe appears as a graceful, organic shape with translucent walls that pulse faintly with bioluminescent light. Its interior contains a symbiotic culture that actively filters and preserves food particles suspended in water, creating a nutrient-rich gel that can extend rations indefinitely. The mycelium breathes, contracts, and relaxes in slow rhythms, almost like a sleeping heartbeat.

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook of Sera, Evening Shift Harvester, The Pearl

The light through the dome has turned copper. Soon the artificial sun will dip below the inverted canopy and the mist will rise. I sit on the edge of my terraced plot, knees dusty, hands still carrying the clean smell of cut stems.

My Myco-Carafe sits beside me on the wooden rail. It glows soft green, the way it always does when it is digesting. I unscrew the cap and pour in the last of today's whey, the leftover liquid from the protein vats down in the Ring. The mycelium hums a note lower, accepting the offering.

The carafe cost me three months wages. Some say it is foolish to spend that much on a vessel when the communal dispensers work fine. They are right, technically. But when I eat from this thing, I taste the grain properly. The flavors do not blur together like they do with synthesized rations. It is as close to Earth as any of us will ever come.

I close my eyes and let the gel dissolve on my tongue. Warm. Earthy. Alive.

Tomorrow we begin the new cycle. But tonight, this is enough.
