ASSET: Pulse Cord (Emergency Life-Line)

Three meters of biosynthetic rope woven from The Pearl's root mycelium. One end clips to a worker's belt harness; the other terminates in a hardened steel carabiner for emergency anchor points. The cord pulses green when tension is healthy, amber under strain, red when the wearer exceeds safe drift threshold. Standard issue for all Root Weave maintenance crews.

LOGBOOK ENTRY:

Logbook kept by Mira Solheim, Maintenance Technician, Third Shift

Root Weave, Junction 17-Alpha

Year 847 After Launch

---

The air tastes like recycled copper and old sweat. It always does down here.

I clip my Pulse Cord to anchor point C-7 and feel the familiar warmth as mycelium fibers settle against my palm. Alive. Like holding hands with the ship itself. My headlamp cuts a white cone through the humidity while the sounds of The Gut breathing reach up through the grating.

The tunnel walls curve away in both directions—stalactites of infrastructure reaching down like the fingers of some sleeping god. I check my cord before every step. Six hundred times I've walked these paths.

Oris flagged a thermal variance in Sector 17-Alpha. I am the whisper's answer.

The cord's green glow pulses in time with my heartbeat. I match my breathing to its rhythm as I edge forward. Somewhere ahead, the ship is losing heat. Somewhere down here, a wound is opening.

I find it seventeen minutes later: a hairline crack in junction housing 17-A-4, weeping superheated vapor into the dark. I seal it with biofoam. The green light steadies.

Good. The ship breathes easier now.

I rest my forehead against the cold alloy and listen to the Gut settling around me. In four hours, the day shift will take over. In four hours, I will climb back through the Root Weave to the City, to my apartment, to the smell of cooking and my daughter's laughter.

But right now, in this moment, it is just me and the ship. And the cord holds.
